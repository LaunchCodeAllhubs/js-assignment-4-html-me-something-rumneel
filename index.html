Kadai Paneer Recipe

An Exquisite Harmony of Spice and Flavor in a Scrumptious Indian Curry

If you're looking for a delicious and spicy Indian curry to enjoy, I recommend trying " kadai paneer recipe." It's a popular dish from the northern region of India and is known for its fiery flavor. Here's a recipe you can follow to make it at home:

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Paneer measured thawed
  • 2 Onion cubed
  • 3 Tomatoes chopped
  • 1 Capsicum cubed
  • 1 tablespoon Ginger finely chopped or julienne
  • 2 Green chillies slit
  • 1.5 teaspoon Red chilli powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds powder
  • 1 teaspoon Garam masala powder
  • ¼ teaspoon Turmeric powder
  • Salt
  • ½ teaspoon Kasoori methi
  • 3 tablespoon Coriander leaves chopped

To Temper

  • 2 tablespoon Oil or butter
  • 1 teaspoon Coriander seeds crushed roughly

Directions

  1. Heat oil in a pan/kadai and temper it with coriander seeds, either crushed roughly or whole.
  2. Fry ginger quickly after adding it to the pan.
  3. Introduce chopped onions and slit green chillies, frying until the onions turn transparent.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and salt to the mixture.
  5. →For kadai masala powder recipe, please click here!
  6. Incorporate all the powders - chili powder,kadai masala powder, garam masala, and turmeric.
  7. Continue frying until the tomatoes become gooey and the oil starts to ooze out. Then, add cubed capsicum and fry for 1 minute, ensuring the crunchiness and color remain intact.
  8. Add paneer cubes (previously soaked in hot water)and crush kasoori methi between your palms,adding it to the dish.
  9. Pour ½ cup water and mix well. Cook until the masala coats the paneer evenly and the gravy thickens to a nearly dry consistency (not too dry).
  10. Finally, add chopped coriander leaves and transfer the dish to the serving plate. Enjoy the deliciousness!

Notes :

  • Keep paneer immersed in hot water until before you cook.
  • Cook capsicum without changing its crunchiness.
  • You can add ginger garlic paste in place of ginger.Use one tsp.
  • Cook the whole process in high flame except while cooking tomatoes.
  • Use fresh paneer for best results instead of frozen.
